HKDSE Exam Challenge

Long Question Format Marks: [4]

A student constructs a chemical cell using a Silver half-cell (Ag rod in \( AgNO_3(aq) \)) and a Magnesium half-cell (Mg rod in \( Mg(NO_3)_2(aq) \)).

To complete the circuit, the student uses a piece of filter paper soaked in Potassium Chloride (\( KCl(aq) \)) as the salt bridge. Initially, a voltage is recorded, but it drops to zero almost immediately.

(a) Explain why the voltage drops to zero rapidly. [2 marks]

The \( Cl^- \) ions from the salt bridge react with the \( Ag^+ \) ions in the silver half-cell to form an insoluble precipitate of silver chloride (\( AgCl(s) \)). [1]
This precipitate blocks the flow of ions through the salt bridge, breaking the circuit. [1]

(b) Suggest a suitable chemical that should be used to soak the filter paper instead of \( KCl(aq) \), and state the direction of flow of its anions. [2 marks]

Potassium nitrate (\( KNO_3(aq) \)) / Sodium nitrate (\( NaNO_3(aq) \)). [1]
The anions (e.g. \( NO_3^- \)) will flow towards the Magnesium half-cell (the anode/negative electrode). [1]
