Common Misconceptions

"Electrons flow through the salt bridge to complete the circuit."

The Right Concept:

Electrons NEVER flow through solutions or the salt bridge. Electrons only flow through the external solid conductors (wires and electrodes). The circuit is completed by the flow of mobile ions (cations and anions) through the salt bridge and electrolytes.

"The salt bridge produces the electrical energy."

The Right Concept:

The electrical energy is generated by the spontaneous redox reaction occurring at the electrodes (due to the difference in reduction potentials). The salt bridge simply acts as a conduit to balance charge and close the loop.

"Cations flow to the Anode, Anions flow to the Cathode."

The Right Concept:

It's the exact opposite! A helpful mnemonic is:
- Anions flow to the Anode (to neutralize newly formed positive ions).
- Cations flow to the Cathode (to replace consumed positive ions).
